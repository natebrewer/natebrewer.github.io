Antweight BattleBot

Quick Specifications

  • Weight class: Antweight UK
  • Weapon: Vertical spinner
  • Drive: N20 brushed motors
  • Battery: 450 mAh 2S LiPo
  • Materials: PLA/TPU frame, steel spinner

Mechanical Design

The main structural pieces of this bot are printed from high-infill PLA to take advantage of PLA's increased rigidity. PLA pieces are shown in white on the model currently. The walls, however, are printed out of TPU. TPU is a type of plastic known as an elastomer, which can stretch and flex without experiencing permanent deformation. This is ideal for small-scale battlebot armor, as TPU can withstand hits which would shatter PLA pieces. The tradeoff is that the flexibility makes TPU non-ideal for maintaing structure. Therefore, I get the best of both worlds from the design combination I am currently running. The rigid PLA top and bottom plates provide structral strength where they are unlikely to take direct hits from other bots, while the flexible TPU walls absorb these hits. Mechanically, this robot is a 20-wheel drive vertical spinner design. I have a spinner made from 1/8th inch bar steel functioning as the kinetic weapon.
